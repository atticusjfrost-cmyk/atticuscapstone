SPRING MEMORIES

The sun rises up from the hills and I walk across the wildflower fields.

They are tall and broad and reach up to the sky. Pink and purple stain my hands.

I come here every spring. Every spring the flowers are in bloom and every spring I've been asked how I changed, but I say them I'm just a singular road.

Parmenides said that nothing can come from nothing. And, if nothing can come from nothing, then nothing begins and nothing ends. All is one, unchanging, being. The flowers bloom here each year; in the light that falls unchanged.

There is a slight humming amongst it all. It tugs at me and I am pulled away.
